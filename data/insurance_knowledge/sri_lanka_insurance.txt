SRI LANKA INSURANCE INDUSTRY — COMPREHENSIVE KNOWLEDGE BASE

== REGULATORY FRAMEWORK ==

INSURANCE REGULATORY COMMISSION OF SRI LANKA (IRCSL)
— Established under the Regulation of Insurance Industry Act No. 43 of 2000
— Previously known as Insurance Board of Sri Lanka (IBSL) until 2011
— Under the purview of the Ministry of Finance
— Mandates: licensing, supervision, solvency monitoring, consumer protection

KEY LEGISLATION
— Regulation of Insurance Industry Act No. 43 of 2000 (as amended)
— Insurance (Amendment) Act No. 27 of 2007
— Insurance Industry (Amendment) Act No. 23 of 2011 (created IRCSL from IBSL)
— Motor Traffic Act: makes third-party motor insurance compulsory

LICENSING CLASSES (post-2011 separation requirement)
— Long-Term Insurance (Life): Manages life, health, and savings products
— General Insurance: Manages motor, fire, marine, liability products
— Composite insurers had to segregate by 2015

SOLVENCY REQUIREMENTS
— Minimum required capital: LKR 500 million (life), LKR 500 million (general)
— Risk-Based Capital (RBC) framework introduced progressively
— Insurers must maintain required solvency margins at all times

== MOTOR INSURANCE IN SRI LANKA ==

COMPULSORY THIRD-PARTY (CTP) INSURANCE
— Required under Motor Traffic Act
— Must be obtained before registering/renewing vehicle at Department of Motor Traffic (DMT)
— Covers: death, bodily injury, property damage to third parties
— Does NOT cover own vehicle or own injuries
— Tariff: regulated by IRCSL; same rate across all insurers
— Certificate of Insurance issued; must be carried in vehicle

COMPREHENSIVE MOTOR INSURANCE
— Includes CTP + Own Damage (OD)
— OD covers: accident damage, fire, natural disasters (flood, storm)
— Additional covers: Theft, key replacement, road assistance
— Sum insured based on market value at renewal; not replacement value

NO-CLAIM DISCOUNT (NCD) — Sri Lanka
— Claim-free Year 1: 10% discount
— Year 2: 20% discount
— Year 3: 30% discount
— Year 4: 40% discount
— Year 5+: 50% discount (maximum)
— NCD is portable between insurers (with NCD certificate)
— Making a claim resets NCD to 0% next renewal

MOTOR CLAIMS PROCESS — SRI LANKA
1. Report to insurer within 24–72 hours (check policy)
2. Submit claim form + driving licence + registration book
3. For accidents: obtain police report from nearest police station
4. For theft: provide police complaint + FIR
5. Vehicle inspection by insurer's surveyor
6. Repair at approved garage (cashless) or reimburse approved amount
7. Depreciation applies to parts based on vehicle age

== HEALTH INSURANCE IN SRI LANKA ==

MARKET STRUCTURE
— IRCSL regulates health insurance under Long-Term (life) class
— Government provides free healthcare via state hospitals (high usage)
— Private health insurance complements state coverage

COMMON PRODUCTS
— Individual Hospitalization Plans
— Family Floater Plans
— Group Medical — employer-provided for corporate staff
— Critical Illness (Cancer, Cardiac, Stroke, Kidney Failure, etc.)
— Personal Accident (PA) Plans

KEY FEATURES IN SRI LANKA
— Room Rent Limits: Policies specify maximum daily room rent (e.g., LKR 3,000–10,000/day)
— Sub-limits on specific treatments (cataract, dialysis, maternity)
— Pre-authorization required for planned admissions
— Network hospitals: cashless settlement at empanelled hospitals
— Reimbursement: For non-network hospitals
— Waiting Period: 30 days general, 2 years pre-existing conditions
— Maternity: Usually excluded for first 2 years; sub-limited thereafter

== LIFE INSURANCE IN SRI LANKA ==

POPULAR PRODUCTS
— 20-Year Endowment: Most popular savings + protection product
— Term Life (Pure Risk): Low premium, death benefit only
— Whole Life: Lifelong cover + cash value
— Education Plans: Endowment with benefit timed to child's education milestones
— Retirement Annuity Plans
— Unit-Linked Insurance Plans (ULIPs)

POLICY LOAN
— Policyholders can borrow against endowment cash value (up to 80–90% of surrender value)
— Interest charged; unpaid loans reduce death benefit

FREE LOOK PERIOD
— 14 days from receipt of policy document
— Policyholder can return policy for full refund (minus medical expenses)

PREMIUM PAYMENT
— Annual, semi-annual, quarterly, monthly
— Most insurers accept bank transfer, cheque, direct debit, online payment
— Grace period: 30 days for annual/semi-annual; 15 days for monthly

SECTION 54 — IRCSL ACT
— Insurers must credit bonus to participating life policies
— Declared bonuses form part of sum assured on maturity/death

== TAKAFUL (ISLAMIC INSURANCE) ==

— Amana Takaful Lanka PLC is the main Takaful operator in Sri Lanka
— Shariah-compliant insurance: policyholders contribute to a mutual fund (Tabarru)
— Surplus in the fund is returned to participants (not profit for insurer)
— Wakalah model: insurer charges management fee
— Available for life, health, motor, fire, and marine

== CLAIMS — SRI LANKA SPECIFIC ==

LIFE INSURANCE CLAIMS
Documents Required:
— Claim form (insurer-specific)
— Original policy document
— Death certificate (from Registrar General's Department)
— Birth certificate of deceased
— NIC (National Identity Card) of claimant
— Medical certificate / hospital records (for illness deaths)
— Police/post-mortem report (for accidental/unnatural deaths)
— Nominee identification documents

Settlement Timeline: IRCSL mandates settlement within 30 days of complete documents

MOTOR CLAIMS
— Surveyor must inspect WITHIN 24 HOURS of intimation for total loss
— 3rd Party claims: Lodge with insurer + police; may take months if disputed
— Motor Accident Claim Tribunal (MACT): Handles 3rd party dispute resolution

REPUDIATION GROUNDS (common)
— Material misrepresentation at proposal stage
— Violation of policy conditions (e.g., driving without valid licence)
— Claim for excluded cause
— Fraudulent claim

GRIEVANCE REDRESSAL
— First: Insurer's internal complaints mechanism (must respond within 14 days)
— Second: IRCSL Consumer Affairs Division (Tel: +94 11 2394 478)
— Third: Insurance Ombudsman (www.insuranceombudsman.lk)

== SRI LANKA INSURANCE MARKET DATA ==

Market Penetration: ~1.2% of GDP (below global average of ~7%)
Premium Volume: Approx. LKR 280 billion (2023 estimates)
Life : Non-Life split: ~60:40
Number of licensed insurers: Approximately 28 companies
Distribution channels: Agents, brokers, bancassurance, direct (online)

== AGENTS AND BROKERS ==

INSURANCE AGENTS
— Individual agents must hold IRCSL agent licence
— Training: CPIII (Certificate of Proficiency in Insurance Industry)
— Agents represent ONE insurer only

INSURANCE BROKERS
— Independent; represent the client, not the insurer
— Must hold IRCSL broker licence
— Can place business with multiple insurers
— Higher professional standards required

BANCASSURANCE
— Banks distribute insurance products as agents
— Major channels: HNB (HNB Assurance), DFCC (Union Assurance), Sampath Bank, BOC, People's Bank

== TAX TREATMENT ==

Premium Tax Relief (Sri Lanka)
— Life insurance premiums: up to LKR 100,000 per year qualifies for income tax deduction
— Under Section 34 of Inland Revenue Act No. 24 of 2017 (as amended)
— Health insurance premiums: allowable deduction under employer benefits

== KEY IRCSL CONSUMER RIGHTS ==

As a policyholder in Sri Lanka, you have the right to:
1. Receive a policy document within 30 days of first premium
2. Receive a free-look period of 14 days
3. Receive a benefit illustration before purchasing a ULIP or endowment
4. Have your claim settled within 30 days of submitting all required documents
5. Lodge a complaint with IRCSL if your insurer fails to respond within 14 days
6. Access an Insurance Ombudsman for independent dispute resolution
7. Know all exclusions, waiting periods, and conditions BEFORE purchasing
